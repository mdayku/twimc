# Demand Letter Template

## Structure Guidelines

A demand letter should contain the following sections:

### 1. Recipient Information and Date
- Date of letter
- Recipient name and address
- RE: Brief subject line

### 2. Introduction
- Identify the sender/plaintiff
- State the purpose of the letter
- Brief overview of the claim

### 3. Statement of Facts
- Chronological narrative of events
- Include specific dates, amounts, and relevant details
- Cite supporting documentation
- **CRITICAL**: Only include facts that can be proven or documented

### 4. Legal Basis / Liability
- Explain why the recipient is liable
- Reference relevant laws, regulations, or contract terms
- Connect the facts to the legal theory
- Keep it concise but clear

### 5. Damages
- **Special Damages**: Quantifiable economic losses (bills, receipts, lost wages)
- **General Damages**: Non-economic losses (pain, suffering, inconvenience)
- Provide breakdown with supporting documentation

### 6. Demand and Deadline
- Clear statement of demanded relief (usually monetary amount)
- Reasonable deadline for response/payment (typically 14-30 days)
- Statement of intent to pursue legal action if demand not met

### 7. Exhibits
- List all supporting documents
- Number them for easy reference
- Note which facts each exhibit supports

## Tone and Style

- **Professional and firm**, not emotional or threatening
- **Factual and specific**, avoid vague accusations
- **Concise**, typically 2-4 pages
- **Well-organized**, use clear headings and numbered paragraphs

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Exaggeration or overstating the case
- Including unsubstantiated allegations
- Emotional language or personal attacks
- Unreasonable demands or deadlines
- Vague or ambiguous language
